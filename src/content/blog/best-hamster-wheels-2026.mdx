## Why Your Hamster Needs the Right Wheel

Hamsters are natural runners. In the wild, they can cover up to **5 miles per night** foraging for food. A quality exercise wheel isn't a luxury — it's essential for your hamster's physical and mental health.

The wrong wheel can cause back problems (especially wheels that are too small), foot injuries from wire mesh surfaces, or keep you awake all night with squeaking. We tested the most popular options to find wheels that are safe, quiet, and properly sized.

## How We Picked These Wheels

We evaluated hamster wheels based on four criteria:

- **Size**: Syrian hamsters need at least 8" diameter; dwarf hamsters need 6.5"+
- **Noise level**: Ball-bearing designs vs. axle-based
- **Surface safety**: Solid running surfaces prevent bumblefoot
- **Stability**: Heavy bases that won't tip during enthusiastic midnight runs

## Hamster Wheel Size Guide

Getting the right size is the single most important factor. A wheel that's too small forces your hamster to arch their back while running, which can cause spinal problems over time.

| Hamster Type | Minimum Wheel Diameter |
|---|---|
| Dwarf (Roborovski, Campbell's) | 6.5 inches |
| Chinese Hamster | 8 inches |
| Syrian (Golden) Hamster | 8–10 inches |

**When in doubt, go bigger.** No hamster was ever harmed by a wheel that was too large.

## What to Avoid

- **Wire mesh wheels**: Feet and toes can get caught, causing serious injury
- **Cross-bar wheels**: Hamsters can trip and get hurt
- **Wheels under 6 inches**: Too small for any adult hamster
- **Hamster balls**: Not a substitute for a wheel — they're stressful and poorly ventilated
